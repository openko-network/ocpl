CONDITIONS

1. Protocol Openness

   Any software that implements OpenKO protocols must remain interoperable
   with the canonical OpenKO protocol specifications published in the rfcs/
   and specs/ directories of the canonical repository. You may not use
   technical measures, patent claims, contractual restrictions, or any other
   mechanism to prevent third parties from implementing these specifications
   or from federating with nodes that run your software.

2. Identity Sovereignty

   You may not modify this software to prevent users from:

     a. Exporting their DID documents and cryptographic key material in full.
     b. Exporting their contribution history, reputation scores, attestation
        records, and mission participation data in a machine-readable open format.
     c. Migrating their identity and all associated data to another compatible
        node at any time without penalty or delay.
     d. Revoking all delegations and reclaiming full agency over their agents,
        capsules, and capability tokens.

3. Data Portability

   Any hosted service operating this software must, upon a user's written
   request, provide a complete machine-readable export of all data associated
   with that user's DID within 30 days, at no charge.

4. No Lock-in

   You may not enforce any patent, trade secret, license term, or contractual
   restriction that would prevent a third party from:

     a. Implementing the OpenKO protocol specifications.
     b. Building software that reads or writes OpenKO data formats.
     c. Building a node that federates with nodes running your software.
     d. Forking this software and operating an independent network.

5. Constitutional Invariants

   You may not modify this software to circumvent or remove enforcement of
   the constitutional principles defined in RFC 0001:

     i.   No permanent central authority
     ii.  Protocols remain open
     iii. Participation remains voluntary
     iv.  Contributions remain transparent
     v.   Identities remain sovereign
     vi.  Knowledge remains portable
     vii. Governance remains federated
     viii.AI systems remain auditable
     ix.  Local autonomy preserved
     x.   Public-good missions prioritized

6. Attribution

   All distributions must retain this license notice. User-visible
   documentation and hosted interfaces must credit the OpenKO project.

7. Patent Non-Aggression

   Any contributor who holds patents that would be infringed by a conforming
   implementation of the OpenKO protocol specifications grants a royalty-free,
   perpetual, irrevocable patent license to all parties for such implementation.

8. Source Availability for Network Services

   If you operate a publicly accessible service that uses a modified version
   of this software, you must make the complete corresponding source of your
   modifications available to users of that service under the terms of this
   license.

COMPLIANCE

If you cannot comply with these conditions, you may not distribute or
operate this software.

## Changes in v1.1

- **Condition 1**: Generalized "canonical OpenKO protocol specifications published in the rfcs/ and specs/ directories of the canonical repository" to "the authoritative specification repository designated by the licensor"
- **Condition 5**: Embedded the 10 constitutional invariants directly; removed reference to external "RFC 0001"
- **New: Dependency Boundary section**: Explicitly states OCPL does not propagate to linked libraries
- **New: License Compatibility section**: Names compatible inbound licenses (MIT, Apache 2.0, GPL v2+, LGPL, AGPL, MPL 2.0, etc.)
- **New: Outbound Compatibility section**: Clarifies distribution as GPL v3 / AGPL v3 combined works
- **New: Extensions section**: Documents the OCPL-Format, OCPL-Tool, OCPL-Model extension system
- **Preamble**: Added to explain purpose and intent
- **Condition 6**: Added "lineage record is permanent" language
- **Condition 7**: Added "survives any transfer of the patent" language
